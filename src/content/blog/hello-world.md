Every developer eventually builds their own site. This is mine — again.

I've had personal sites before. They've mostly been forgotten, un-updated shrines to whatever I was working on at the time. This one is different (I promise, I say every time).

## Why now

I'm at an interesting inflection point in my career. After years of building products at various companies, I'm actively looking for what's next. That means thinking hard about what kind of work I find meaningful, what kind of teams I thrive in, and what I actually want to spend the next few years on.

Writing is how I think. So: a blog.

## What I'll write about

Mostly engineering craft — the things I've learned building products, the mistakes I've made, the patterns I keep reaching for. Some career stuff. Occasionally something more personal.

No content calendar. No SEO strategy. Just writing when I have something worth saying.

## The stack

This site is built with [Astro](https://astro.build), styled with plain CSS and Tailwind, and hosted on Netlify. All content is markdown files checked into git. No database, no CMS, no drama.

It's fast, it's simple, and writing a new post is just creating a new `.md` file. That's the whole workflow.
